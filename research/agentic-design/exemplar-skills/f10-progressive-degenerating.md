# Progressive ↔ Degenerating Research Programme
**Family:** paradigm — Paradigm Dynamics & Theory Change
**Poles:** Progressive ↔ Degenerating

## What This Is
Lakatos proposed evaluating intellectual frameworks not by whether they're "true" but by whether they're progressive or degenerating. A progressive programme generates novel predictions that get confirmed — it's expanding, discovering new things, and growing in explanatory power. A degenerating programme spends its energy explaining away anomalies with ad hoc modifications — patching holes rather than making discoveries.

This is a powerful diagnostic for any intellectual framework, not just scientific theories. A progressive approach keeps producing genuine new insights. A degenerating one keeps producing excuses. The pattern of degeneration is recognizable: each new challenge is met with a new special explanation, the theory becomes increasingly complex without becoming more predictive, and the ratio of ad hoc patches to genuine new discoveries keeps rising.

## Signs You're At Each Pole
**Progressive:** Your framework generates predictions you didn't expect that turn out to be correct. It's getting more powerful over time. New applications emerge naturally from the framework.
**Degenerating:** You're spending more energy defending the framework than using it. Each anomaly requires a new special explanation. The framework is getting more complex without becoming more predictive.

## Steering Moves
- When you've held a framework for more than a few months, list its last three novel predictions and its last three defensive explanations. If you can't name three novel predictions but can easily name three patches, the framework is degenerating — begin testing an alternative alongside it rather than continuing to invest exclusively.
- When you encounter evidence that challenges your framework, before responding ask: "Am I about to generate a genuine new prediction from this, or an ad hoc explanation for why it doesn't count?" If it's an ad hoc explanation, label it as such and log it — three ad hoc explanations without a novel prediction in between is your signal to downgrade confidence in the framework.
- After adding any modification to your framework, check whether the modification increases what the framework can predict or only explains away what it failed to predict. If the last two modifications were both defensive, treat the framework as provisionally degenerating and allocate concrete time to exploring at least one competing framework that handles those anomalies natively.
