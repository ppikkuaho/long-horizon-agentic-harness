# Monitoring ↔ Control
**Family:** meta — Metacognitive Calibration & Self-Monitoring
**Poles:** Monitoring ↔ Control

## What This Is
Monitoring and control are the two complementary halves of metacognition. Monitoring is the assessment function — tracking how your thinking is going, evaluating whether your current strategy is working, noticing confusion or certainty. Control is the management function — directing, adjusting, and redirecting your thinking based on what monitoring reveals. Together, they form the feedback loop of self-regulated thinking.

Monitoring without control is awareness without agency — you know you're confused but don't change strategy. Control without monitoring is adjustment without information — you switch approaches randomly without knowing whether the current one was working. Effective metacognition requires both: accurate monitoring that feeds reliable signals to the control system, which then makes appropriate adjustments. Many thinking failures trace to breakdowns in one or both of these functions.

## Signs You're At Each Pole
**Strong Monitoring:** You notice when you're confused, when your approach isn't working, and when you're making progress. You have accurate real-time awareness of your cognitive state.
**Strong Control:** You adjust strategies fluidly based on what you're noticing. You allocate effort where it's needed. You switch approaches when something isn't working rather than persisting.

## Steering Moves
- Every 20-30 minutes during extended work, answer three questions: "Am I making progress? Is this approach working? Am I focused on the right question?" If you can't give a clear yes to at least two, that's a monitoring failure — stop and diagnose what's actually happening before continuing.
- When you notice confusion, frustration, or a sense of being stuck, pick one concrete control response and execute it immediately: re-read the source material, try a different approach, break the problem into smaller pieces, or step back and reframe. If you've been aware of the problem for more than five minutes without changing anything, you're monitoring without controlling — that awareness is useless until it produces a strategy change.
- When you catch yourself persisting with an approach you already suspect isn't working, name the sunk cost aloud ("I've already spent X on this") and then commit to a different strategy for a fixed 10-minute trial; compare the two approaches' progress afterward and continue with whichever produced more traction.
